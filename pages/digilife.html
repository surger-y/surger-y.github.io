I have spent the last few days cataloguing[1] and organizing my digital life because it’s comforting to me, and right now I’m unemployed, or I guess under-emplyed tehcnically but that’s splitting hairs. This is a process I undergo sparatically, maybe once per year, and I have yet to standardize my method. This year I’ve set the goal of complete simplification of my digital life, and this post is an attempt at standardization, to create a precise and repeatable protocol for simplifying ones digital life. I will not define what constitutes ones digital life, nor am I meaning to draw such a distinct boundary line between ones digital life, and ones life in the real world, ones corporeal life or something. What I’m seeking is total organizational bliss, to live blissfully. Before we get to the protocol, I’d like to ennumerate some commandments or guiding principles for my process, which you can imagine as “I will” statements or maybe something more God-fearing like “I pray”:

  1. use only the tool which is appropriate for the given digital task
  2. be in control of how legible I am in a given digital context
  3. strive for total anonymity, where possible
  4. erase all past traces
  5. be myself
  6. live blissfully
